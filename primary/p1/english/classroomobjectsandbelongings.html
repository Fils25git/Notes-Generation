UNIT 6: CLASSROOM OBJECTS AND PERSONAL BELONGINGS

Lesson 6.1: Identifying Places

We learn names of places in school.


  • Classroom
  • Playground
  • Library
  • Toilet

Short dialogue:

Teacher: Where is the classroom?
Child: It is here.

Teacher: Where is the playground?
Child: It is outside.

Exercise:

1. Point to the classroom and say: This is the classroom.

2. Point to the playground and say: This is the playground.

3. Match the place with the correct word:

PlaceWord
InsideClassroom
OutsidePlayground
QuietLibrary

Lesson 6.2: How Many…?

We ask about number of objects.


Examples:

How many chairs? → 3 chairs
How many books? → 5 books


Exercise:

1. Count the pencils in your desk: _______
2. Count the books on the table: _______
3. How many windows are in your classroom? _______

Lesson 6.3: Yes or No

We answer simple questions with Yes or No.


Short dialogue:

Teacher: Is this a pen?
Child: Yes.

Teacher: Is this a chair?
Child: No.

Exercise:

1. Is this a bag? _______
2. Is this a book? _______
3. Point to an object and ask your partner: Is this a ______?

Lesson 6.4: Children Play a Game in Class by Asking Questions

Children take turns asking and answering simple questions about objects.


Exercise:

1. Ask: What is this? Answer: This is a _______
2. Ask: How many chairs? Answer: _______
3. Ask: Is this a pen? Answer: Yes / No

Lesson 6.5: Sounds and Spellings

Practice sounds of letters in classroom words.


Examples:

c – chair
b – book
p – pen


Exercise:

1. Say the first letter of each word: chair, book, pen
2. Point to the object and say the word aloud
3. Match letters with words:

LetterWord
cChair
bBook
pPen
