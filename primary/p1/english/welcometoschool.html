UNIT 1: WELCOME TO THE CLASSROOM

Greeting children in the classroom

When we meet people, we greet them.
Greeting means saying hello politely.

We say Good morning in the morning.
The morning is before lunch.

We say Good afternoon after lunch.
We say Good evening when the sun is going down.

In school, we greet our teacher and friends every day.

Short dialogue:

Teacher: Good morning, children.
Children: Good morning, teacher.

Another dialogue:

Child: Good afternoon, teacher.
Teacher: Good afternoon.

Exercise:

1. Say Good morning to your friend.
2. Say Good afternoon to your teacher.
3. Act the dialogue with your partner.

Introducing myself

When we meet new people, we tell them our name.

We say My name is... to introduce ourselves.

We can also say how old we are.

Example:
My name is Anna.
I am six years old.

Short dialogue:

Teacher: What is your name?
Child: My name is John.

Exercise:

1. Say your name.
2. Tell your age.
3. Introduce yourself to the class.

Introducing others

Sometimes we introduce our friends to other people.

We use the words This is...

Example:
This is my friend Peter.

Short dialogue:

Child: This is my friend Mary.
Teacher: Nice to meet you, Mary.

Exercise:

1. Introduce your friend.
2. Say: This is my friend...
3. Point to a friend and introduce him or her.

Giving and responding to instructions

Teachers give instructions in class.
Instructions tell us what to do.

Common instructions:
Stand up.
Sit down.
Listen.
Open your book.
Close your book.

We must listen and do what the teacher says.

Short dialogue:

Teacher: Stand up.
Children: (They stand up.)

Exercise:

1. Stand up.
2. Sit down.
3. Follow the teacher’s instructions.

Naming objects

In the classroom, we see many objects.
We learn their names.

Examples of classroom objects:
Book
Pen
Pencil
Desk
Chair
Blackboard

We can point and name the object.

Short dialogue:

Teacher: What is this?
Child: It is a book.

Exercise:

1. Point to a book.
2. Name three objects in your class.
3. Say: This is a pen.

Sounds and spellings

Letters make sounds.
We listen carefully and repeat the sounds.

Example sounds:
b – book
c – chair
p – pen

We spell words using letters.

Exercise:

1. Say the sound of b.
2. Spell the word book.
3. Match letters with objects.

End of unit assessment

Now we check what we have learned.

Answer the questions:
1. How do you greet your teacher?
2. Say your name.
3. Name two classroom objects.

Activity:
Greet your friend.
Introduce yourself.
Follow one instruction.
